NORMAL UNO The classic card game. First player to empty their hand wins. 2–10 players.

⚡ Quick Start
1
Each player is dealt 7 cards. The remaining cards form the draw pile.
2
Flip the top card to start the discard pile. Play begins clockwise from the host.
3
On your turn, play a card matching the color or value of the top discard card.
4
If you can't play, draw one card from the pile and your turn ends.
5
When you have one card left, tap the UNO! button before playing it!
6
First player to play all their cards wins the round! 🏆

📋 Key Rules
🚨
UNO Call: You must tap UNO! when you play your second-to-last card (leaving 1). If you forget and another player challenges you, you draw 2 penalty cards.
⚔️
Challenge UNO: Tap any player's chip to challenge them. If they have 1 card and didn't call UNO, they draw 2!
🔄
Draw Stacking: Draw 2 and Wild Draw 4 can be stacked. If you receive a draw penalty, you must take all stacked cards unless you counter with another draw card.
♻️
Deck Reshuffle: When the draw pile runs out, the discard pile (except the top card) is shuffled and becomes the new draw pile.
💡 Pro tip: Save Wild Draw 4s for when you're desperate — play color-matching cards first to keep your Wild cards as insurance. Watch opponents with few cards and be ready to challenge!
UNO FLIP Two-sided cards — light and dark. Playing a Flip card switches the entire board between sides. Dark side rules are brutal.

⚡ How It Works
1
Game starts on the Light Side. Normal UNO rules apply with one addition: the Flip card.
2
When a Flip card is played, all players flip to the Dark Side of their hand. Only dark-side cards may be played.
3
Another Flip card flips back to the Light Side. You can flip back and forth throughout the game.
4
Dark side cards are more powerful — Draw 5 instead of Draw 2, and brutal special cards.

🌈 Light vs Dark Colors
☀️
Light Side: Red · Yellow · Green · Blue — classic UNO colors.
🌙
Dark Side: Pink · Teal · Orange · Purple — four entirely new colors. They don't mix with light-side colors.
⚠️
Important: You can only play cards matching the current side. Light cards cannot be played on the dark side and vice versa.
⚠️ Flip Strategy: If the dark side is hurting you, saving a Flip card to switch back to the light side can be a lifesaver — but it also lets your opponents play their light cards again!

⚡ Special Rules
💀
Elimination: Any player who accumulates 25 or more cards is immediately eliminated from the game. Their cards are removed and play continues.
📚
Mega Stacking: Draw 2, Draw 6, Wild Draw 4, and Draw 10 can ALL be stacked on each other freely. A full stack can easily reach 20+ cards.
🃏
Discard All Win: Playing Discard All when you have exactly 1 card remaining is an instant win — even without calling UNO first.
🎯
Last Player Standing: If all other players get eliminated, the surviving player wins automatically.
💀 Danger Zone: Keep your hand under 20 cards at all times. Once you hit 22+, a single Draw 6 from an opponent can eliminate you instantly. Prioritize playing draw cards to pass the pain forward!
💡 Survival tip: Discard All is your escape hatch — hold it until you have 10+ cards and play it to reset. Wild Roulette is best saved for when you want chaos, not when you're already losing.
